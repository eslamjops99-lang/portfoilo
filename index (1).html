About Me

I'm Eslam Nabil Mohamed, an Aerospace and Mechanical Engineering student at Zewail City of Science and Technology — Egypt's foremost research institution, founded by Nobel Laureate Ahmed Zewail.

My engineering focus sits at the intersection of propulsion systems, flight dynamics, and control theory. I'm driven by the challenge of translating abstract physics into systems that actually fly — whether that's a microjet engine machined in the lab, a pitch autopilot tuned on root locus plots, or a rocket interception scenario solved in MATLAB.

I approach every project with an engineer's discipline: rigorous analysis first, then simulation, then hardware. I believe aerospace pushes mathematics and creativity to their absolute limit — and that's precisely why I chose it.

My long-term goal is to contribute to advanced propulsion development or autonomous flight systems at a world-class aerospace organization.

Microjet Engine Design & Fabrication
Full design-to-fabrication project spanning thermodynamic cycle analysis, SolidWorks CAD for all turbomachinery components, and hands-on manufacturing of a single-shaft turbojet. The engine architecture includes a centrifugal compressor, annular combustor, and axial turbine stage. Currently in the precision machining phase with compressor and turbine blades under production.
